## LinkedIn Post 1

Date collected: July 7, 2026  
Post link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samdunning_if-i-was-a-saas-cmo-wanting-to-crack-ai-search-activity-7411157214385553408-IZQG

### Summary

This post explains how a SaaS CMO can approach AI search by focusing on the strongest areas of the product, mapping buyer search behavior, and building content around use cases, comparisons, alternatives, integrations, and high-intent buying moments. Sam Dunning emphasizes that AI search visibility should be connected to pipeline, not just traffic.

### Key Insight

The key insight is that AI-powered SEO content production should start with customer research and buyer intent. B2B SaaS companies need content that answers how prospects compare vendors, solve expensive problems, evaluate alternatives, and understand specific use cases. This makes content more useful for both human buyers and AI-driven search systems.

## LinkedIn Post 2

Date collected: July 7, 2026  
Post link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samdunning_yesterday-google-leaked-that-ai-search-activity-7461452124535717888-LN3A

### Summary

This post discusses Google's guidance that strong SEO fundamentals still matter for AI search. Sam Dunning highlights the importance of helpful, reliable, people-first content, clear organization, useful images and video, and avoiding overreliance on shortcuts such as special markup or artificial AI SEO hacks.

### Key Insight

The key insight is that AI search does not replace good SEO fundamentals. For AI-powered SEO content production, teams should create non-generic, helpful, well-structured content that reflects real customer needs. The priority should be quality, clarity, usefulness, and trust rather than chasing quick AEO or GEO tricks.

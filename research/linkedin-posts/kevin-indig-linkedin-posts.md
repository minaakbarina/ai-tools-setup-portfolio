## LinkedIn Post 1

Date collected: July 7, 2026  
Post link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_in-seo-we-obsess-over-where-we-rank-but-activity-7423732576139476992-sx3r

### Summary

This post discusses why winning in AI Search requires moving beyond traditional keyword targeting. Kevin Indig explains that brand perception in AI systems may be shaped by the content that ranks well, such as content hubs, quote pages, keyword-focused assets, and other SEO-heavy materials. The post highlights that brands need to understand how LLMs form brand associations and how search-visible content can influence those associations.

### Key Insight

The key insight is that AI-powered SEO content production should focus on shaping brand understanding, not only ranking for keywords. B2B SaaS companies need content that clearly explains who they serve, what problems they solve, what makes them different, and why they are trustworthy. In AI search, content can influence how LLMs describe, compare, and recommend a brand.

## LinkedIn Post 2

Date collected: July 7, 2026  
Post link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_backlinks-still-matter-in-ai-search-just-activity-7378865408218394624-JiPA

### Summary

This post discusses how backlinks still matter in AI search, but not in the same way as traditional SEO. Kevin Indig explains that authority helps, but the relationship is not simply linear. He highlights that backlink quality matters more than quantity and that authority signals can influence how brands are mentioned across AI systems such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity.

### Key Insight

The key insight is that AI-powered SEO content production should be supported by authority-building activities. Publishing content alone is not enough. B2B SaaS companies need credible mentions, high-quality backlinks, digital PR, trusted references, and useful content that earns recognition across the web. AI visibility depends on both content quality and external authority signals.
